First of all, a better splash screen&nbsp;— what is it with those weedy mushrooms?!&nbsp;😎
</p>

<p>
Next, my plug-ins and scripts&nbsp;— these were the main motivation for installing <a href="https://github.com/ivan-hc/GIMP-appimage">GIMP Hybrid</a> in the first place, and I already had a few tucked away on an old backup drive:
</p>

- TransBg¹
- [Cyan](https://github.com/rodlie/cyan)
- [Rysynthersizer](https://github.com/bootchk/resynthesizer)

Installing these was as straightforward as it gets; it was just a case of copying them over into the relative directories:

`~/.config/GIMP/2.10/plug-ins/` 
`~/.config/GIMP/2.10/scripts/`

 

Then came the tricky bit — tracking down and installing the ones I've somehow managed to lose:

- [Export Layers](https://github.com/kamilburda/gimp-export-layers/)
- [Liquid Rescale](https://github.com/bzed/gimp-plugin-registry/tree/master/lqr)
- [Refocus](https://github.com/JoesCat/gimp-refocus-plugin)

Luckily, I managed to find a [build for ExportLayers](https://github.com/kamilburda/gimp-export-layers/releases) in kamilburda's Git repo. The others, though, were a little more challenging as both are written in C and need to be compiled&nbsp;— and, because I'm running everything from an AppImage, even that was too much messing about for my liking.

However, I did find a simpler solution; I grabbed the [gimp-plugin-registry](http://archive.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/pool/universe/g/gimp-plugin-registry/gimp-plugin-registry_9.20180625ubuntu2_amd64.deb) package from the Ubuntu archive, extracted everything, and pulled both plugins out of the mix from there.²

I absolutely hate bloat, but I may well end up adding a few more plug-ins and scripts from this collection&nbsp;— some of them actually look quite useful. 

Happy days, young Bob!&nbso;👍
