Oh, I do so much like to customise things. And why not? After all, there's nothing quite like a bit of kit that matches your needs perfectly. So, I've been busy pimping my recent installation of GIMP Hybrid!

First of all, my plug-ins and scripts&nbsp;— these were the main motivation for installing [GIMP Hybrid](https://github.com/ivan-hc/GIMP-appimage ) in the first place, and I already had a few tucked away on an old backup drive:

- TransBg¹
- [Cyan](https://github.com/rodlie/cyan)
- [Rysynthersizer](https://github.com/bootchk/resynthesizer)

Installing these, then, was as straightforward as it gets; it was just a case of copying them over into the relative directories:

`~/.config/GIMP/2.10/plug-ins/` 
`~/.config/GIMP/2.10/scripts/`

 

Next came the tricky bit — tracking down and installing the ones I don't seem to have:

- [ExportLayers](https://github.com/kamilburda/gimp-export-layers/)
- [LiquidRescale](https://github.com/bzed/gimp-plugin-registry/tree/master/lqr)
- [Refocus](https://github.com/JoesCat/gimp-refocus-plugin)

Luckily, I managed to find a [build for ExportLayers](https://github.com/kamilburda/gimp-export-layers/releases) in kamilburda's Git repo. The others, though, were a little more challenging as both are written in C and need to be compiled&nbsp;— and, because I'm running everything from an AppImage, even that isn't a straight forward task.

However, I did find a simpler solution; I grabbed the [gimp-plugin-registry](http://archive.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/pool/universe/g/gimp-plugin-registry/gimp-plugin-registry_9.20180625ubuntu2_amd64.deb) package from the Ubuntu archive, extracted everything, and pulled both plugins out of the mix from there.

Happy days, young Bob. Happy days.
