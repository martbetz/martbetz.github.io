There's an age-old saying that goes something like this: "If it isn't broke, don't fix it!" I'd like to expand on that: "If it _is_ broke, try not to fix it by breaking anything else!"

We all know the score by now; so many useful plugins and scripts that used to work in GIMP 2.X are completely incompatible with 3.X, and I've whole boatload of custom stuff that I'm not about to throw overboard! Fear not, though, young Bob&nbsp;— there's a very simple solution!

[GIMP Hybrid](https://portable-linux-apps.github.io/apps/gimp-hybrid.html) is a specific (experimental) "flavor" of GIMP that's based on GIMP 2.X and distributed as an AppImage — this, of course, means I can run it alongside my 3.X installation without any conflicts or issues, and I can fire it up whenever I need to use my goodies!

This isn't the first time I've used this approach; I use an older (2012) version of Hugin, for example, to help create my custom lens-correction profiles for Lensfun (I just can't do it the same way in layer versions because the whole method was overhauled).

Anyway... onwards an upwards&nbsp;— or _backwards_, as the case may be...
