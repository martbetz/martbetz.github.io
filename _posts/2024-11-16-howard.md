I'm saddened and shocked to learn that the world of broadcasting has lost one of its most comforting and articulate radio presenters and podcast hosts: Howard Hughes. 

​While his mainstream radio career was undeniable, a completely new generation came to know Howard through his independent digital labor of love: The Unexplained with Howard Hughes.​ 

Long before podcasting became the multi-billion-dollar industry it is today, Howard recognized the power of the internet to connect with a global niche audience. For over sixteen years, he transformed his show into a world-class hub for discussions on everuthing from fringe science to the paranormal.

​What truly set The Unexplained apart from other paranormal shows was Howard's background as a serious journalist. He approached the bizarre and the unusual not with cheap sensationalism, but with open-minded curiosity and profound respect. Whether he was interviewing a world-renowned physicist or an ordinary person who had witnessed something extraordinary, Howard treated everyone with the exact same dignity.

​For his dedicated global community of listeners, Howard’s podcast was more than just entertainment; it was a sanctuary. During lonely nights, stressful times, or quiet commutes, his familiar voice offered an escape into a world of wonder. He made the vastness of the cosmos feel a little less intimidating and a lot more fascinating.

​Howard frequently closed his broadcasts by urging his audience to "stay safe, stay calm, and above all, stay in touch." It wasn't just a catchy catchphrase; it was a genuine reflection of how much he valued the people on the other side of the speaker.

​Though his microphone has gone quiet, the massive archive of interviews and broadcasts Howard left behind ensuring his voice will live on. He spent a lifetime asking the big questions and looking up at the stars. For those of us left listening, the airwaves feel a little emptier without him&nbsp;— but the legacy of his warmth, curiosity, and flawless craft will remain entirely unforgettable.
