Buying cheap tools is rarely a good idea, but several hundred quid for drivers? 😲 Surely there must be a middle ground? Well, as it turns out, there is — but you really need to know what to look for and where you need to look to find it.

When choosing a watchmaker’s screwdriver set, quality is king; the only problem is, quality comes at a price — and the price of a quality set these days can just be plain darn crazy!

The good news is, quality drivers do exist at reasonable price points; the bad news is, they’re scarce; especially in a market saturated by cheap and nasty rubbish.

So how do you sort the wheat from the chaff? Well, have a read of this: Best Watchmaker Screwdrivers for Beginners by Melvin Hollenburg (wahawatches.com).

Thank’s to Melvin’s advice, I was able to narrow things down. Two close contenders were a nine-piece set by Cousins UK (S57442) and a five-piece set by KD89 Horotec (MSA01.095),¹ but I finally settled on a seven-piece set by Beco Technic (205941).

They’re winging it in from Germany as we speak, and all for the price of £35 (including postage and packing) — so be sure to check back for an update, Bob!

¹ I’ve listed these particular ones as per the seller’s description, though I highly doubt that Horotec has much at all to do with them (as far as I’m aware, none of their stuff’s made in France). Even so, they could still be a sensible option.

Update (Dec 13, 2024): Hold the fort! The delivery turned up today, and I’m really disappointed — as soon as I opened the package, I could tell that something was wrong. 🤨

Although there were no signs of damage, the drivers looked awkwardly placed; then, when I peeled off the heat-wrap, the lid of the box just sprang open! 😣

Hello, SOLID STONE FLOOR! Please meet my DELICATE FRIENDS!

The drivers don’t fit in their case; it’s plainly as simple as that — would a brand like Beco Technics really miss something like this, or have we a swap-out for knock‑offs? 🤔

I don’t have imperforate faith in these, so I’ll simply have to return them. I may well try the Cousins set — these ones come with a nice little stand, and they work out a bit cheaper.
