This webpage is currently under construction. I was going to create a ‘name and shame’ page, here, but I’d still be hard at it when the stars burn out; that being the case, I've instead decided to share some of my better experiences.

Linux on smartphones and tablets and desktops, bright shiny lenses with really low ƒ-stops, custom-made keyboards with smooth thocky springs: these are a few of my favourite things!

I’ve been told I’m hard to please, but I’d have to argue to the contrary; all I ask for is good value — I expect what I was promised, on time, every time, and at the right price (and not to be taken for an idiot by excepting anything less).

Please keep in mind that I’m neither affiliated with any of these products or services, nor am I providing any guarantees; all of my comments, thoughts, and opinions are purely based on personal experience — so I won’t be held responsible for your teeth exploding, Bob!

Grip-Seal Poly Bags

These are an absolute necessity; nothing that's anywhere near as cost effective will keep your gear organised, free from dust and dirt, and protected against moisture (or even a downright drenching) — Get some, and get them NOW!

Storage Containers

Good quality storage boxes are truly indespensible, especially when it comes to keeping your gear safe and dry; polypropylene boxes are particularly handy because they're also suitable for storing documents and prints.

Really Useful Products, for example, offer an impressive range of solutions; all of their boxes are very well made, relatively inexpensive, stackable, and have a nice rectangular interior (they're also available in many different sizes and a choice of several colours). I ordered mine from Hobbycraft; checkout was hastle free, delivery was lightning fast, and the packaging was second to none.

Power Banks

Batteries will always need charging, so a power bank’s simply a must – but with so many options to pick from, take time to consider needs!

I currently use the Vurac 20,000 mAh power bank (I chose to go with the white one, but there’s also a version in black); when paired with a USB charger, I can ready a spare on the go.¹

Computer Cables

For price and speed of delivery, I haven't found a more reliable supplier than Kenable; from experience, I honestly can't recommend them enough.

LSE Transfer Tape

When it comes to repairing and refurbishing gear, I really couldn’t live without this stuff; when working with low surface-energy materials, it’s an absolute must — it’s a far better solution than liquid adhesives for so many reasons!

I tend to use 300LSE by M3; it’s far from cheap and it’s difficult to get hold of, but it’s certainly one of the best options out there.

Wrist Watches

It’s hard to “ƒ/8 and be there” if you don’t even know what time it is! Both my snapper and my phone have a perfectly good clock, so I haven’t worn a wrist watch for quite a few years; recently, though, I’ve started to use them again.

Here’s the thing: my phone’s tucked away from theiving little hands, and the clock on my snapper distracts me; all it takes now is a hassle-free glance — and I don’t need to charge up the battery every two hours!

My current go-to beater is a Casio AE-1500; it’s inexpensive, reliable, well made, and has a large clear display that I can easily read in bright sunlight.

Water Shoes

Apart from wet weather, nothing ruins my day like sore feet; reliable footware is an absolute must (especially when you're out and about with a camera), and water shoes are a great (if somewhat unconvential) option.

The best I've found so far are Hot Tuna Aqua Shoes; they're well made, comfortable, and comparatively well priced. I ordered mine from Sports Direct (I snagged two pairs for under £20, which also included postage), and I honestly couldn't fault them for their speedy delivery.

Boonie Hats

Boonie hats are a god send; they may seem unflattering to some, but — unlike that melted nose on your face, there, Bob! — at least you can take them off when the sun goes down. Just about every military force in the world provides them as standard issue (and for very good reason).

My favourite one to date is the Jaxon & James boonie (it's a little less Rambo and a little more Lara Croft), and it's saved my skin — quite literally! — on countless occasions. I ordered mine from Village Hats, but lots of different places tend to stock them (the putty version is particularly nice).

Earplugs

I always carry earplugs; your hearing is important, and you never know when you'll find yourself in a noisy situation (I once got very drunk at a nightclub and passed out next to a loudspeaker; I damaged my hearing permanently, and the frequent bouts of tinnitus lasted several — very miserable! — years).

¹ A word of caution: I strongly suggest reading The Great Battery Brawl by Dom Varney before you even think of doing the same — at best, this approach is risky; if you don't know what you're doing, it can be downright dangerous!
