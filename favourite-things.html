This webpage is currently under construction. I was going to create a ‘name and shame’ page, here, but I’d still be hard at it when the stars burn out; that being the case, I've instead decided to share some of my better experiences.

Linux on smartphones and tablets and desktops, bright shiny lenses with really low f-stops, custom-made keyboards with smooth thocky springs; these are a few of my favourite things!

I’m told I’m hard to please, but I’d have to argue on the contrary; all I ask for is good value — I expect to get what’s promised, on time, every time, and at the right price; I won't be taken for an idiot by excepting anything less.

Please keep in mind that I’m neither affiliated with any of these products or services, nor providing any guarantees; all of my thoughts and opinions are purely based on personal experience — so I won’t be held responsible for your batteries exploding, Bob!

Storage Containers

In general, the best storage containers I've found are made by Really Useful Products; they’re moulded from polypropylene, translucent (though semi-translusent versions are also available), roomy (without being excessively bulky), very sturdy and well made, have a nice rectangular interior, relatively inexpensive, and are available in a huge variety of shapes and sizes.

Hobbycraft have never let me down, here; ordering is hastle free, delivery has always been well priced and lightning fast, and their packaging is second to none.

Computer Cables

For price and speed of delivery, I haven't found a more reliable supplier than Kenable; from experience, I honestly can't recommend them enough.

Water Shoes

Apart from wet weather, nothing ruins the time I spend with my camera more than sore feet; reliable footware is an absolute must, and water shoes are a great (if somewhat unconvential) option. The best I've found so far are Hot Tuna Aqua Shoes; they're well made, comfortable (they fit perfectly), and comparatively well priced.

I purchased mine from Sports Direct (I snagged two pairs for under £20, which also included postage), and I couldn't fault them for their speedy delivery and straight-foward ordering process.

Boonie Hats

Boonie hats are a god send; they may look a little rediculous, but — unlike that melted nose on your face, there, Bob! — at least I can take them off when the sun goes down. Just about every military force in the world provides them as standard issue, and for damned good reason.

My favourite so far is my Jaxon & James boonie in kaki, and it's saved my skin — quite literally! — on countless occasions; it's also a little less Rambo and a little more Lara Croft (which is nice). I ordered mine from Village Hats, but quite a few different places tend to stock them.
